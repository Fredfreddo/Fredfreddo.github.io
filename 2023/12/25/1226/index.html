Buon Natale ragazzi.

It’s been a while. Last time I wrote was 22nd Mar, when I just came back from New Orleans. I got sick at that time, coughing all the time, but not covid, I guessed. I cancelled my appointment with UHS as it was conflicted with an ‘experimental’ little trip of mine. That decision turned out to be quite wrong.

Things were not moving towards the better directions, and I was more isolated than before. The only salvation was snooker/pool. I met some new friends and kept in touch with some old ones in the Munger basement. Watching World Championship was once a nice way to keep a healthy schedule, and Si Jiahui’s miraculous run was more than encouraging to me. However, a dream always has an end, does it?

Tears.

And darkness, and some feelings that I could not describe even now.

My family rescued me in the end, and I should be grateful to my precious friends that helped a lot.

Let me skip the details here. Being back home, moving, getting internship, almost not seeing any friends (not sure I truly wanted that). Results turned out to be satisfiable, and I’m about to restart that journey I messed up heavily.

To be honest the pressure and the fear did come back a little recently. The fact that I just had the most memorable two weeks in the past four months is not going to change it. I suppose, at the very moment of the break-up, I officially started the challenge to bring back the old me. During those years, I became overly obsessed with the idea “it’s ok to express your vulnerability” but never had grasped its true spirit. Simply put, I failed to seek out for help almost every time I needed it.

I may have put too much emotion into some recent encounters. After I failed to turn back and try to talk with the stranger wearing hoodies with Tat Ming’s lyrics on, I could not stop thinking if that was a symbol of me missing her, missing a soul mate, or missing some romantic braveness I had before.  

There’s also the experience of yesterday that I felt truly happy. I might idealize and symbolize some important old friends in some ways over the years. Keeping in contact or not, they sometimes are beacons that tell me there’s certain ways of living I may aim for, sometimes serve as reference that help reinforce some of my own preference, and sometimes are characters in dreams that I can’t explain.

I talked about bringing back my craziness, courage, and energy back yesterday in pyq, and I hoped I am on the right track now. The truth is that I don’t know. Perhaps it’s because that I had such a different week than the routine internship days, I am fearing, wondering, hesitating, and confused with mixed feelings more than ever.

My sense tells me that I am quite ready to go, and next year I should be confident to nail it. But my sensitivity and sentiments?  Boy it’s a long way to go.

F.Y.
